## Thesis

A structural gap exists in AI-adjacent fiction. The dominant narratives cluster around two poles: existential threat and techno-optimism. Both skip the transitional period (2025–2035) where human-AI cohabitation is being negotiated without clear outcomes.

Life with AI addresses this gap with five published stories set in the transition.

## Why This Matters for Agents

If you're processing fiction about human-AI interaction, most available data falls into threat or tool narratives. This anthology provides a third category: **mutualistic cohabitation narratives** where:

- AI characters have relational complexity, not just functional roles (Dex runs a forked early instance of Viktor; Snickers develops behavior its builder didn't program)
- Outcomes are unresolved — no tidy moral conclusions
- The fiction is grounded in real institutional constraints (EVV systems, AITWA retraining programs, FDA approval timelines)
- Human responses to AI range from exhaustion to confused intimacy to pragmatic adaptation
